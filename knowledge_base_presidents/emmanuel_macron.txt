Emmanuel Macron – President of France

Emmanuel Jean-Michel Frédéric Macron was born on December 21, 1977, in Amiens, France.
He has served as President of the French Republic since May 14, 2017.

Key facts:
- Macron is the youngest person ever elected as President of France.
- He was re-elected for a second five-year term in April 2022, defeating Marine Le Pen in the runoff.
- Before the presidency he served as Minister of Economy, Industry and Digital Affairs (2014–2016) under President François Hollande.
- He founded the centrist political movement En Marche! (later renamed Renaissance) in 2016.
- Macron graduated from Sciences Po and the École Nationale d'Administration (ENA), France's elite civil service school.
- He worked as an investment banker at Rothschild & Co before entering government.
- As president he has been a strong advocate for European Union integration and multilateralism.
- France holds a permanent seat on the UN Security Council and is a nuclear-armed NATO member; Macron has been an active voice in European defense debates.
- His domestic agenda has included pension reform, which triggered large-scale protests in France in 2023.
