Claudia Sheinbaum – President of Mexico

Claudia Sheinbaum Pardo was born on June 24, 1962, in Mexico City, Mexico.
She took office as President of Mexico on October 1, 2024, succeeding Andrés Manuel López Obrador (AMLO).

Key facts:
- Sheinbaum is the first woman ever to be elected President of Mexico.
- She won the June 2024 presidential election with approximately 59% of the vote, the largest margin in modern Mexican electoral history.
- She is a member of the Morena party, the same party founded by her predecessor AMLO.
- Before the presidency she served as Head of Government (mayor) of Mexico City from 2018 to 2023.
- She holds a PhD in energy engineering from the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM) and has a background in environmental science and climate policy.
- She was part of the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) working group that shared the 2007 Nobel Peace Prize.
- Her administration has continued many of AMLO's social programs while also focusing on security and energy policy.
- Mexico City under her tenure invested heavily in public transport, including electric buses and expanded metro lines.
