Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva – President of Brazil

Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva, known as Lula, was born on October 27, 1945, in Caetés, Pernambuco, Brazil.
He has served as President of Brazil since January 1, 2023, having previously served two terms from 2003 to 2010.

Key facts:
- Lula is the only Brazilian president to have been elected to three presidential terms.
- He won the October 2022 presidential election, narrowly defeating incumbent Jair Bolsonaro by about 1.8 percentage points — the closest result in Brazilian history.
- He co-founded the Workers' Party (Partido dos Trabalhadores, PT) in 1980, growing out of the trade union movement.
- During his first two terms (2003–2010) Brazil's economy grew strongly, and tens of millions of Brazilians were lifted out of poverty through programs such as Bolsa Família.
- He was imprisoned in 2018 on corruption charges stemming from the Lava Jato (Car Wash) investigation, but the Supreme Court annulled his convictions in 2021, restoring his political rights.
- Lula left school after the fourth grade and worked in factories as a teenager; he became a metalworkers' union leader in São Paulo in the 1970s.
- His current administration has prioritized environmental policy, pledging to end deforestation in the Amazon by 2030, and social spending programs.
- Brazil under Lula has positioned itself as a leader among Global South nations and hosted the G20 summit in November 2024.
